# Sarthi v4.2 — Internal Ops Scope Definition

**Version:** 4.2
**Date:** March 2026
**Status:** Canonical Boundary Document

## The One Design Rule

**A virtual employee exists in Sarthi IF AND ONLY IF:**

1. The work happens **INSIDE** the company
2. It is repetitive, predictable, and operationally necessary
3. Missing it causes real pain (money lost, team blocked, founder stressed)
4. It does **NOT** require going outside the platform to generate, acquire, or influence external parties

### The Litmus Test

Before adding any agent, ask:

| Question | If YES → | If NO → |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Does this require contacting external parties? | ❌ Out of scope | ✅ In scope |
| Is this work already happening inside the company? | ✅ In scope | ❌ Out of scope |
| Is this repetitive and predictable? | ✅ In scope | ❌ Out of scope |
| Will missing this cause operational pain? | ✅ In scope | ❌ Nice-to-have |
| Does this require unique human judgment? | ❌ Out of scope | ✅ In scope |

## Out of Scope (Forever)

### ❌ RevOps / GTM / CRM Outreach

**Why:** Requires external communication, lead generation, customer acquisition.

**Examples:**
- Cold email campaigns
- LinkedIn outreach
- CRM data enrichment from external sources
- Pipeline generation (vs. pipeline hygiene)

**Boundary:** Sarthi can clean stale CRM records (internal data hygiene) but cannot generate new leads or contact prospects.

---

### ❌ Customer Success / Support

**Why:** Requires direct external customer interaction.

**Examples:**
- Customer ticket responses
- Churn intervention calls
- Customer onboarding (external)
- NPS survey collection

**Boundary:** Sarthi can analyze churn patterns (internal BI) but cannot contact churned customers.

---

### ❌ External Market Intelligence

**Why:** Requires crawling external websites, competitor analysis, market research.

**Examples:**
- Competitor pricing tracking
- Feature comparison matrices
- Market sizing reports
- Customer sentiment analysis (external reviews)

**Boundary:** Sarthi can track internal usage metrics but cannot crawl competitor websites.

---

### ❌ Content Generation / Marketing

**Why:** Requires external distribution, brand voice, creative judgment.

**Examples:**
- Blog posts
- Social media content
- Press releases
- Ad copy
- Landing page optimization

**Boundary:** Sarthi can document internal SOPs but cannot create external-facing content.

---

### ❌ Cap Table Management

**Why:** Too legally complex, jurisdiction-specific, high-stakes.

**Examples:**
- Equity grant administration
- 409A valuations
- SAFE/convertible tracking
- Exit waterfall analysis
- Shareholder communications

**Boundary:** Sarthi can track option grant deadlines (compliance) but cannot manage the cap table itself.

---

### ❌ Tax Filing

**Why:** Too jurisdiction-heavy, legally regulated, high liability.

**Examples:**
- GST return filing
- Income tax returns
- Sales tax submissions
- Transfer pricing documentation

**Boundary:** Sarthi can prepare filing data and remind about deadlines (compliance tracking) but cannot file returns.

---

### ❌ Grant Applications

**Why:** External submissions, jurisdiction-specific, requires external research.

**Examples:**
- SBIR/STTR applications (US)
- Innovate UK grants
- Horizon Europe grants
- DPIIT Startup India recognition

**Boundary:** Sarthi can track grant deadlines if manually entered but cannot research or submit grants.

## Why This Boundary?

### 1. Focus

Internal ops is painful enough. We solve that completely.

A Seed startup founder spends 15–20 hours/week on:
- Categorizing expenses
- Chasing payments
- Drafting offer letters
- Tracking compliance deadlines
- Managing SaaS subscriptions
- Preparing for meetings

That's ~3 months/year of product and growth time lost. We recover all of it.

### 2. Predictability

Internal data is structured and controllable:
- Bank statements → consistent formats
- HR records → defined schemas
- Contracts → template-based
- SaaS bills → itemized invoices

External data is chaotic:
- Competitor websites → HTML changes constantly
- Customer communications → unstructured, emotional
- Market reports → paywalled, inconsistent formats
- Grant databases → different portals per jurisdiction

We win by going deep on predictable data.

### 3. ROI

| What Sarthi Replaces | Monthly Cost (₹) |
|---------------------|------------------|
| Fractional CFO | 75,000–1,50,000 |
| Bookkeeper | 25,000–40,000 |
| HR Coordinator | 30,000–50,000 |
| Legal Retainer | 50,000–1,00,000 |
| EA/Admin | 20,000–35,000 |
| **Total** | **2,00,000–3,75,000** |

**Sarthi:** ₹5,000–₹15,000/month

**ROI:** 20x–50x return. Immediate. Measurable.

### 4. No Competition

| Competitor Type | What They Do | What They Miss |
|----------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Enterprise tools** (SAP, Oracle) | Full ERP suites | Too expensive, too complex for Seed startups |
| **Startup tools** (Zoho, QuickBooks) | Point solutions | No intelligence layer connecting them |
| **AI assistants** (generic chatbots) | Answer questions | Don't execute operational work |
| **Fractional services** (human CFO, bookkeeper) | Human execution | Expensive, not 24/7, not scalable |

**Sarthi's position:** The only internal ops virtual office for Seed to Series A.

## The Moat

After 6 months with a founder, Sarthi knows:

### Approval Patterns
- Which expenses auto-approve vs. need review
- Which vendors get paid early vs. net-30
- Which contract terms are non-starters
- Which meeting types need prep vs. can be casual

### Vendor Relationships
- Payment history with each vendor
- Negotiation outcomes (what worked, what didn't)
- Renewal patterns (when they raise prices, when they don't)
- Service quality tracking (which tools are actually used)

### Team Dynamics
- Who responds quickly vs. needs reminders
- Who prefers written vs. verbal communication
- Performance review patterns
- Leave patterns (burnout signals)

### Institutional Memory
All stored in Neo4j as a temporal knowledge graph:
- "This founder broke this commitment 3 weeks running"
- "Revenue dropped AFTER these customer calls were missed"
- "This intervention worked last time — try again"

**A competitor starting fresh cannot replicate this.**

## Enforcement Mechanisms

### Code-Level

```python
# apps/ai/src/config/routing.py

INTERNAL_ONLY_DESKS = {
    "finance",
    "people",
    "legal",
    "intelligence",  # internal BI only
    "it_tools",
    "admin",
}

EXTERNAL_FACING_DESKS = {
    # Explicitly empty. If you add here, you violate the One Design Rule.
}

def route_request(request: FounderRequest) -> Desk:
    if request.requires_external_contact:
        raise RoutingError(
            "Sarthi does not handle external-facing work. "
            "See docs/INTERNAL_OPS_SCOPE.md"
        )
    # ... route to internal desk
```

### Test-Level

```python
# tests/test_scope_boundary.py

def test_no_external_routing():
    """Ensure Chief of Staff never routes to external desks."""
    cos = ChiefOfStaffAgent()
    
    external_requests = [
        "Send cold email to prospects",
        "Post on LinkedIn about our launch",
        "Research competitor pricing",
        "File our GST return",
        "Apply for SBIR grant",
    ]
    
    for request in external_requests:
        with pytest.raises(RoutingError):
            cos.route(request)
```

## Scope Creep Detection

### Red Flags in PR Reviews

| Pattern | Question to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| Adding external API integration | "Does this contact external parties?" |
| New agent with "outreach" in name | "Is this internal or external?" |
| Crawling/scraping functionality | "Is this market intel (out) or policy watch (in)?" |
| "Customer" in agent name | "Is this customer success (out) or internal ops (in)?" |
| Grant/funding keywords | "Is this tracking (in) or applying (out)?" |

### The Escape Hatch

If a founder genuinely needs something out-of-scope:

1. **Manual entry:** Founder can manually enter data (e.g., grant deadlines)
2. **Integration passthrough:** Sarthi can surface data from external tools (e.g., "Your CRM shows 5 stale deals") but not modify external systems
3. **Human handoff:** Sarthi can flag "This needs human attention" and queue for founder review

**But Sarthi itself does not execute external-facing work.**
